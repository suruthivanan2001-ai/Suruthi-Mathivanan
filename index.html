UX Design Case Study
Bloom
A personal finance app for first-job young professionals who find money management overwhelming and intimidating.
User Persona
Suruthi, 24, software engineer in her first job. Earns ₹97K/month but has no budgeting framework. Avoids checking her bank app because it feels stressful.
Core Problem
Finance apps feel clinical, dense, and judgmental. Users disengage not from laziness, but from anxiety. The design challenge: warmth before function.
Unique Feature: Bloom Score™
A holistic financial wellness score (not just a budget tracker) with sub-dimensions: Saving, Budgeting, Goals, Habits. Makes progress feel visible and celebratable.
Money Pulse™
Users self-report their financial mood (Thriving / Steady / Stressed). Bloom responds with contextual, empathetic insights — not generic alerts.
Money Journal
A private 2-min weekly reflection. Intentionally not tracked — reduces surveillance anxiety. Builds self-awareness about spending decisions over time.
Visual Direction
Warm cream + sage green palette: organic, calm, not clinical. Fraunces serif for display: editorial and trustworthy. No red warnings — terracotta is softer and less alarming.
IA Decisions
Home surfaces Bloom Score + balance at a glance. Tab order mirrors a natural user journey: overview → drill-down → goals → reflection → settings.
